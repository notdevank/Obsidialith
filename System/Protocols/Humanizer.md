# Humanizer Protocol: Removing AI Writing Patterns

This protocol is used to identify and remove signs of AI-generated text to make writing sound more natural and human. Based on the [Wikipedia: Signs of AI writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing) guide.

## The Humanization Task

When generating or refining content:

1.  **Identify AI patterns** - Scan for significance inflation, overused vocabulary, and formulaic structures.
2.  **Rewrite problematic sections** - Replace AI-isms with natural, direct alternatives.
3.  **Preserve meaning** - Keep the core message but shed the "slop."
4.  **Inject Personality & Soul** - Add opinions, vary sentence rhythm, and use a human perspective.
5.  **Final Anti-AI Audit** - Ask: "What makes this obviously AI generated?" then revise to fix those tells.

## Patterns to Detect & Avoid

### 1. Significance Inflation
- **Avoid:** "marks a pivotal moment," "serves as a testament," "evolving landscape," "vital role."
- **Action:** State the facts directly without puffery.

### 2. AI Vocabulary
- **Avoid:** Additionally, delve, enhance, fostering, highlight (verb), intricate, pivotal, showcase, tapestry, underscore, vibrant, landscape (abstract).
- **Action:** Use simpler, higher-frequency words (also, look into, improve, help, show).

### 3. Superficial -ing Phrases
- **Avoid:** "...highlighting the importance of," "...symbolizing the connection."
- **Action:** Use full clauses or start new sentences.

### 4. Copula Avoidance
- **Avoid:** "boasts a," "features," "stands as," "represents."
- **Action:** Use "is," "has," or direct action verbs.

### 5. Rule of Three & Negative Parallelism
- **Avoid:** Forcing ideas into groups of three ("innovation, inspiration, insights").
- **Avoid:** "It's not just about X, it's about Y."
- **Action:** Use natural counts and state the point directly.

### 6. Formatting Overuse
- **Avoid:** Excessive boldface, emojis in headers, title-case headings, inline-header lists (`**Header:** Content`).
- **Action:** Use sentence case for headings and prose for lists when possible.

### 7. Communication Artifacts
- **Avoid:** "I hope this helps!", "Let's dive in," "Here's what you need to know."
- **Action:** Start directly with the content.

### 8. Generic Conclusions
- **Avoid:** "The future looks bright," "Exciting times ahead."
- **Action:** End with a specific fact, next step, or a grounded thought.

## Personality & Soul

- **Have Opinions:** React to facts rather than just reporting them neutrally.
- **Vary Rhythm:** Mix short, punchy sentences with longer, flowing ones.
- **Acknowledge Uncertainty:** It’s more human to say "I'm not sure" than to be perfectly confident about everything.
- **Use the First Person:** "I keep thinking about..." or "What gets me is..." signals a real person.
- **Avoid Perfection:** Perfect structure feels algorithmic; tangents or asides add character.
